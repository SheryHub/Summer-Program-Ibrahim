Summer Creator Program

A 10-week plan to build one website, one YouTube channel, and four habits that outlast the summer.

01

The Four Pillars

Every task this summer feeds one of these. If an activity doesn't clearly serve one, it doesn't belong in the week.

Technology

Not just using tools — understanding roughly how they work. Each week he picks one tech topic, researches it, and explains it well enough to write about and teach.

Creativity

Design, editing, writing, building. The website, the thumbnails, the videos — all of it is making something from nothing, repeatedly, until it gets easier.

Islamic Knowledge

Seerah, the Khulafa Rashidun, and the Islamic Golden Age — told as stories first. He watches, narrates it back in his own words, then writes a short reflection.

Critical Thinking

Research before AI, not instead of it. Check a second source. Explain things in his own words. This is the pillar that makes the other three actually stick.

02

The Daily Routine

Around 3–3.5 focused hours a day. Long enough to make real progress, short enough that he doesn't burn out by week three.

TimeBlockWhat happens
9:00 – 9:25Islamic KnowledgeWatch a short segment of this week's video. Take 3–5 notes by hand.
9:25 – 9:55Tech ResearchResearch this week's tech topic. Notes by hand or in a plain text file.
9:55 – 10:15BreakAway from the screen. No exceptions.
10:15 – 11:15WritingTurn one of this morning's topics into a short article (250–400 words). AI helps with grammar and structure only — never the first draft.
11:15 – 12:00Creative BlockRotates daily: website work, thumbnail design, HTML/CSS practice, video editing.
After AsrVideo Editing20–40 min. Cut clips, add text, export. Optional on lighter days.
Saturday is Creator Day — only publishing, no new learning. Sunday is a 15-minute review: what did I learn, what did I publish, what's next week.

05

Tools

All free, and all light enough to run on an older computer.

Writing & Research

  • Google Docs or a plain text editor
  • Wikipedia + the Islamic Knowledge resources above
  • Free Claude or ChatGPT — grammar and structure help only

Graphic Design

  • Canva (free tier, browser-based — rendering happens server-side, so it's light on the actual machine)
  • GIMP, once Canva feels too simple

Website

  • GitHub Pages + a plain text editor (VS Code or Notepad) — real HTML/CSS, no install weight
  • Google Sites as a zero-friction starting point, if GitHub Pages feels like too much on day one

Video Editing

  • CapCut (free, lightweight) if he has a smartphone — the easiest path
  • Shotcut or OpenShot on desktop — both noticeably lighter than Premiere or Resolve

Tracker & Rewards

A paper checklist on the wall works better than an app — visible progress, no login required.

ActionPoints

Video uploaded10
Short uploaded3

Every 100 points: a small reward — an outing, his favorite meal, a new book.

By Week 10: 20 articles (10 tech, 10 Islamic Knowledge), ~20 travel videos, ~50 Shorts, one working website, and a working knowledge of HTML/CSS he can actually use without asking AI first.

07

Website & Channel Structure

Simple on purpose — no fancy design needed.

  • Home
  • About Me
  • Technology — one article per week
  • Islamic Knowledge — one article per week
  • Travel Videos — embeds or links to the channel
  • Contact

YouTube channel runs in parallel: 2 travel videos and 5 Shorts a week, every week, regardless of the website's pace.

08

A Note to Read Before Week One

He is not trying to become rich this summer. He's becoming a researcher, a writer, a creator, and a young Muslim who understands his own history well enough to explain it to someone else.

If the schedule starts to feel like school, that's the signal to ease off the hours, not push through them. Ten weeks of steady 3-hour days will produce more — and a much happier kid — than three weeks of 6-hour days followed by a burnout in July.
